An Hour

A short poem discussing time and its lasting effects. Composed October 12, 2017.

Five hours to sleep, one to shower;
School starts in just an hour.
Too much homework the night before,
too little time to settle the score.

Who would have known: why are we here?
What is the purpose of living in fear?
Be not afraid, they always say --
it's how you make the most of your day.

If we were destined to cream the crops
of a life ever so fruitful that never stops,
it makes me wonder how much time
we've spent it exploring the sublime.

If there's anything I've learned
from the life I deserve,
it's to live it day by day, verse by verse,
blow by blow, curse by curse.

Written and composed by Ben Petrillo, D-Class personnel of the Foundation.
